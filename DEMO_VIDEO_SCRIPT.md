# GyanVriksh — 3-4 Minute Demo Video Script

Read the **NARRATION** aloud; do the **[ON SCREEN]** actions as you speak. Target ~3:40.
Before recording: infra + backend + frontend running, logged out (so you can show login),
and `npm install` done once so the Equipment-QR page works. Keep a phone handy for the QR bit.

---

### Scene 1 — The problem (0:00 – 0:25)
**[ON SCREEN]** Login page (the animated tree) or the guided tour's first chapter.
**NARRATION:**
> "Across India's refineries and chemical plants, a knowledge cliff is coming. The
> engineers who hold decades of hard-won repair wisdom are retiring — and when they
> leave, that knowledge walks out the door with them. It was never written down.
> We built GyanVriksh to fix that."

### Scene 2 — Enter the platform (0:25 – 0:45)
**[ON SCREEN]** Log in as `engineer@bharatchem.in`. Land on the Command Dashboard.
**NARRATION:**
> "GyanVriksh is an on-premise industrial knowledge brain. This is a demo build on a
> synthetic plant dataset — but every part is real: knowledge graph, fine-tuned models,
> and full security. Here's the command dashboard — coverage, equipment health, and a
> live knowledge-cliff risk score."

### Scene 3 — Ask GyanVriksh (0:45 – 1:35)
**[ON SCREEN]** Open **Ask GyanVriksh**. Type or speak:
*"Why do P-101 seals keep failing, and what does OISD-116 say?"*
Let the reasoning trace play; when the answer arrives, click the **speaker** icon.
**NARRATION:**
> "Any worker can just ask — in English, Hindi or Hinglish. Watch it think: it parses
> the question, searches the knowledge graph and documents, and generates the answer.
> Notice every claim carries a citation to the exact document and page — nothing is
> made up. And a technician on the floor can simply listen to it."

### Scene 4 — See the evidence (1:35 – 2:05)
**[ON SCREEN]** Open **Knowledge Graph**. The nodes behind the last answer glow; camera flies in.
**NARRATION:**
> "This is what makes it more than a chatbot. Behind every answer is a living graph —
> equipment, work orders, incidents, people, regulations — and it lights up to show
> exactly which evidence it used. That traceability is what industry needs to trust it."

### Scene 5 — The knowledge cliff radar (2:05 – 2:35)
**[ON SCREEN]** Open **Knowledge Cliff**. Show the risk summary + an expert's Personnel Record.
**NARRATION:**
> "And we tackle the retirement problem head-on. The Knowledge Cliff Radar computes,
> straight from the graph, which experts retire within three years, which equipment
> depends on them, and how much hands-on history is at risk — so you can preserve it
> before it's gone."

### Scene 6 — Field worker + QR (2:35 – 3:05)
**[ON SCREEN]** Open **Equipment QR**; scan a sticker with your phone → the mobile view opens for
that machine → tap a one-tap question → answer reads aloud.
**NARRATION:**
> "For the shop floor, we made it effortless for workers who aren't computer-savvy.
> Print a QR sticker for each machine. Scan it — no typing, no logging in — and get
> one-tap questions about that exact equipment, with the answer spoken back."

### Scene 7 — Preserve expertise (3:05 – 3:25)
**[ON SCREEN]** Open **Preservation Studio**; show the record/expert capsule flow.
**NARRATION:**
> "And when a veteran is about to retire, they record what they know. We transcribe it,
> turn it into a verified knowledge capsule attributed to them, and link it to the
> equipment — so the next generation can still ask the master."

### Scene 8 — Proof + close (3:25 – 3:50)
**[ON SCREEN]** Dashboard Benchmark card (or the PPT benchmark slide).
**NARRATION:**
> "We measured it — a hundred-question benchmark, every answer cited, zero crashes,
> running entirely on-premise at no per-query cost. GyanVriksh: decades of knowledge,
> one question away. Thank you."

---

**Tips**
- If the local LLM is slow, pre-warm it by asking one question before you hit record,
  or run the demo on a GPU / with a GPT-4o key so answers come back in seconds.
- Keep it under 4 minutes; if tight, merge Scenes 7 and 8.
- Speak to the *problem and the outcome*, not the tech stack — judges remember the story.
